How it works Overview

  1. Find nearby volunteers on the site and contact them directly.
  2. Share a GPS pin, vehicle details, terrain, and hazards.
  3. Agree on a safe meet-up plan. Volunteers are neighbors, not pros.

This is a community bulletin board. Use at your own risk.

Posting a request

What information should I include?
  • GPS pin or coordinates
  • Vehicle make/model and whether you have recovery points
  • Terrain and hazards (mud depth, water, snow, slope, rocks)
  • Gear on hand (straps, boards, winch, shackles)
  • How far you are from pavement and gate/land access info
How fast will someone respond?

There are no guaranteed response times. Volunteers help as they are able.

Should I keep my engine running?

Follow safe practices for your vehicle and conditions. If you smell fuel, see water ingress, or the engine is overheating, shut it off and move to a safe area.

For Volunteers

Who can volunteer?

Anyone with basic off-road recovery knowledge and suitable gear. A winch helps but is not required. Good judgment is required.

What gear is recommended?
  • Rated tow/recovery straps and soft shackles
  • Traction boards, shovel, air compressor
  • Recovery points front and rear; winch optional
  • Gloves, lights, and communication (cell or radio)
Can I remove myself later?

Yes. You can request removal or pause your listing at any time.

Legal & Payments

Is this a professional service?

No. This is a volunteer community. We do not supervise, train, or certify anyone.

Does it cost anything?

No fees. Tips or gas money offered by the driver are optional.

Who is responsible for damages?

All arrangements are strictly between drivers and volunteers. By using this site you agree to hold it and its administrators harmless.
