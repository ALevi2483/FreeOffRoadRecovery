Why Volunteer?

  • Help neighbors get home safe when they’re stuck off-road.
  • Build local trail/community connections.
  • Promote safe, responsible recoveries with the right gear and techniques.

Who Can Volunteer?

  • Drivers with off-road experience who understand safe recovery basics.
  • Folks with appropriate gear (tow straps/soft shackles, recovery points, traction boards, winch optional).
  • People willing to follow local laws, land-use rules, and Tread Lightly principles.

What We Ask

  1. No fees. You may accept voluntary gas money only.
  2. Communicate clearly about risks, access, and meet-up safety.
  3. Do not attempt risky recoveries beyond your capability. It’s okay to decline.

Volunteer FAQ

Can I charge for recoveries?

No. This is a volunteer, no-cost community effort. Tips or gas money offered by the driver are optional.

What info will drivers provide?

GPS pin/coordinates, terrain description, vehicle details, hazards, and what gear they already have.

What if I’m unavailable?

Drivers can contact other listed volunteers. You can pause or remove yourself later if needed.

Is this an emergency service?

No. If anyone is in danger, call 911. Volunteers are neighbors lending a hand, not first responders.
